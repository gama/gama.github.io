About

I am a software architect and lead engineer with more than 20 years in the industry. I have a long history working on the design and implementation of complex systems and applications, ranging from applied research in machine learning, computer vision and fintec through mission-critical large scale enterprise software.

At my current position at NVXL, I lead the engineering team responsible for the entire software stack of the company's main product -- a Machine Learning acceleration platform, built upon a multi-FPGA hardware appliance. I oversee and work directly in the design, implementation and deployment of a wide range of components, among which: the OS device drivers; the low-level acceleration libraries; the python-based end-user APIs; the management user-interface; and the infrastructure which supports the automated testing and delivery pipelines.

I am also directly involved in the product's development process, acting as project manager, proposing and introducing agile practises and methods, defining and prioritizing stories, and working closely with marketing, executives and other engineering teams.

Prior to NVXL, I have worked as a software consultant and technical lead at ThoughtWorks, specializing in agile methodologies and lean software processes. I lead a team of 10 engineers responsible for operating a micro-services oriented architecture for the company's main customer (the largest airline in Latin America) and was a member of its principal technical board.

Education-wise, I hold a B.S. and M.S. degree in Computer Science from the Federal University of Minas Gerais in Brazil, and have worked as a research assistant at Rutgers University, NJ.

I am currently located in the city of Belo Horizonte, Brazil and may be reached at . Samples code and contributions may be found on github.at https://github.com/gama.
